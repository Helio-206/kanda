# Demo Readiness

## What works today?

- Landing has the right institutional tone.
- Web routes are correctly split between official operational surfaces and legacy citizen routes.
- Mobile can report, inspect, track, and show a stronger success moment.
- The map is usable as a location and exploration surface.
- Occurrences have status, priority, and timeline storytelling.
- Credibility is visible enough to explain the product direction, even if it is still simple.

## What is missing?

- dashboards still feel too light to pass as real operating consoles;
- map density and cluster communication are not strong enough yet;
- credibility could be richer without becoming complex;
- exploration still needs stronger visual emphasis to feel unavoidable;
- some screens still read like polished prototypes instead of final product surfaces.

## What is critical?

- Make the dashboards feel dense and operational.
- Make exploration feel central, especially on mobile.
- Make the success moment look official and memorable.
- Make the detail screen feel like an active case.
- Make the map tell a territorial story, not just show pins.

## What can wait?

- deeper analytics visuals;
- advanced credibility history;
- more advanced filtering;
- backend integration;
- more complex cluster logic;
- richer admin workflows.

## How to present KANDA in 5 minutes

### 1. Open with the problem

Show the Landing and explain that KANDA is not a citizen complaint app. It is a platform for managing occurrences with AI assistance and community signal.

### 2. Show the AI moment

Open the Mobile report flow and focus on:

- photo capture;
- automated analysis;
- suggested category;
- suggested entity;
- confirm flow.

This is the core “AI does the heavy lifting” moment.

### 3. Show the success moment

After submission, stop on the success screen.

Point out:

- official registration;
- code;
- priority;
- entity suggested;
- next step;
- follow-up action.

This should feel like a real platform event, not a form submit.

### 4. Show the citizen layer

Go to Home, Exploration, and Detail.

Explain that the citizen can:

- discover nearby problems;
- open cases;
- confirm useful occurrences;
- see the impact of participation;
- track the evolution of cases.

### 5. Show the institutional layer

Open the Web dashboards and explain that this is where entities read the territory, prioritize, and manage operations.

That makes the system feel national and operational, not local and app-like.

## Presentation order

1. Landing.
2. Mobile Home.
3. Report flow.
4. AI analysis.
5. Success screen.
6. Detail screen.
7. Map.
8. Web dashboard.

## Final demo line

KANDA is a platform where citizens turn what they see into structured cases, where AI helps classify and route the problem, and where entities get a clearer operational picture of the territory.
