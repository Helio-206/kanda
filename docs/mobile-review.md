# Mobile Review

## Summary

The mobile app is a usable MVP, but it is not yet aligned with the final KANDA product vision.

It still reads more like a self-contained local prototype for reporting and tracking than the citizen-facing half of an institutional platform.

## 1. KANDA Identity

**Score: 6/10**

What works:

- the color palette is broadly close to the Web palette;
- cards, buttons, and badges use a clean, restrained visual language;
- the mobile UI does not feel noisy or decorative;
- the overall tone is serious enough for a civic product.

What does not fully work:

- the typography does not feel like a direct continuation of the Web identity;
- the mobile still has a softer “local utility app” tone in several screens;
- the hero and copy language are not fully institutional;
- badges and cards are acceptable, but they do not yet feel like the same product system as the Web.

Verdict:

The identity is directionally right, but not yet cohesive enough to sell the same product family at first glance.

## 2. Mobile First

**Score: 5/10**

What works:

- the app is clearly usable on a phone;
- the main actions are reachable within a few taps;
- map, report, and profile are present as direct screens.

What does not work:

- the current experience still feels like a local prototype with reporting at the center, not a broader citizen platform;
- there is no strong exploration-first experience;
- credibility is barely visible as a product system;
- the flow feels like “log in, report, check list” rather than “discover, confirm, build trust, follow up”.

Verdict:

It is mobile-shaped, but not fully mobile-first in the product sense.

## 3. Main Flow

**Score: 6/10**

Target flow:

Home -> Report -> AI Analysis -> Confirm Labels -> Success -> Occurrence Detail

What exists:

- Home exists;
- Report exists;
- AI analysis exists;
- occurrence detail exists;
- map selection exists.

What is missing or weak:

- there is no clean dedicated `Confirmar Rótulos` screen in the current flow as a first-class step;
- there is no clear `Sucesso` handoff screen that feels final and trustworthy;
- the flow is more screen-to-screen than product-story-to-product-story;
- the transition from analysis to confirmed submission is not strong enough as a demo moment.

UX issues:

- the app still depends heavily on local state and implicit transitions;
- the flow can feel fragmented because the important steps are not packaged as distinct product moments;
- there is too much “prototype mechanics” visible to the user.

Verdict:

The flow is present, but the product storytelling is still incomplete.

## 4. Explore Occurrences

**Score: 3/10**

This is the biggest gap.

The app does not yet make it obvious that a user can discover nearby problems as a central behavior.

Current issue:

- Home is about the user’s stored occurrences;
- Map is about choosing a point and creating a report;
- there is no strong “explore what is around me” surface;
- the app does not naturally communicate discovery, browsing, or nearby awareness.

Verdict:

This is not yet a central feature. Right now it looks like a map-assisted reporting tool, not an occurrence discovery product.

## 5. Credibility

**Score: 2/10**

What exists:

- a profile screen;
- local counts of saved, open, and resolved occurrences.

What is missing:

- no visible credibility score;
- no credibility timeline;
- no positive or negative credibility events;
- no reinforcement loop for participation;
- no explanation of why the user should care about credibility.

Verdict:

The credibility system is effectively absent in the current mobile experience.

## 6. Occurrences

**Score: 5/10**

What works:

- status badges are clear enough;
- priority badges are readable;
- the detail screen shows the core metadata;
- the local timeline gives some sense of history.

What is weak:

- KANDA Score is not a first-class visual object;
- the responsible entity is not prominent enough;
- the risk communication is still too technical and not productized;
- the status vocabulary is still close to a local prototype, not a polished civic flow.

Verdict:

The occurrence UI is functional, but it does not yet read as a premium product surface.

## 7. Visual Integration with the Web

**Score: 6/10**

Compared with the Web:

- colors are reasonably close;
- card style is compatible;
- spacing is acceptable;
- the mobile is not visually hostile to the Web brand.

Differences:

- the Web feels more institutional and polished;
- the mobile still feels more utility-first;
- typography is not as refined as the Web landing;
- badges and hierarchy are not yet strong enough to feel like one system;
- the mobile lacks the “official platform” feeling that the Web now has.

Verdict:

The mobile is adjacent to the Web identity, but not yet a true continuation of it.

## 8. Demo Readiness

**Score: 5/10**

If we had to present tomorrow:

- the flow would be understandable;
- the app would look real enough to avoid immediate dismissal;
- the jury would see a functioning MVP;
- but the product message would still be muddy.

What the jury would notice:

- report is real;
- map works;
- local persistence works;
- but the credibility story is missing;
- exploration is missing as a core action;
- the app feels like an early prototype instead of a finished hackathon story.

Verdict:

It can be demoed, but it would not yet defend the full KANDA vision with confidence.

## 9. Critical Problems

### Critical

- `Credibility` is not actually present as a product system.
- `Explorar Ocorrências` is not a real central journey.
- The mobile still reads too much like a prototype reporting app, not the citizen half of the official KANDA platform.

### Important

- The main flow is incomplete as a narrative.
- There is no strong success moment after submission.
- KANDA Score is not visually elevated enough.
- The app still depends on the user understanding too much of the local prototype mechanics.
- The mobile and Web do not yet feel like a single branded system with different responsibilities.

### Can wait

- deeper polish on empty states;
- richer visual hierarchy in profile;
- more refined spacing on secondary screens;
- future credibility timeline design.

## 10. Final Verdict

- Is the mobile aligned with the KANDA vision? **Partially, but not enough yet.**
- Does the mobile feel like an institutional and serious product? **Only in fragments.**
- Does it feel like a natural continuation of the Web? **Not yet.**
- What should be fixed first before the hackathon? **Credibility, exploration, and a stronger citizen story around reporting, confirmation, and follow-up.**

## Bottom Line

The mobile is usable, but it is still carrying too much of the old prototype DNA.

Right now it is a local reporting app with some KANDA styling. It is not yet the mobile half of the official KANDA product.

If we present this as-is, the strongest impression will be:

- “there is something working”

not:

- “this is a serious institutional platform with a strong citizen layer.”
